Come! Let's discover the hidden meanings of Nav Durga!

First Form:

Shailaputri is the first form of Navdurga. When the mind is wavering, chanting the name of this Devi Shailaputri helps the mind to be centered and committed

Second Form:

Brahmacharini is the second form of Navdurga.When we pray to this form of Mother Divine, we invoke the quality Brahmacharya. And our consciousness starts moving in the infinity, in our true nature

Third Form:

Chandraghanta is the third form of Navdurga.When the qualities of alertness and steadfastness arises, then the mind is like an adornment. Such a mind adds beauty to oneâ€™s nature.

Fourth Form:

Kushmanda is the fourth form of Navdurga.As Kushmanda, the Devi contains the entire creation within her. She is the Devi who can give you the highest prana (creative energy).

Fifth Form:

Skandamata is the fifth form of Navdurga.Skanda is the skillful one. Often when one is very skillful, they tend to be arrogant. Most of very talented people have arrogance. But here the skill is combined with a humility that nurtures innocence.

Sixth Form:

Kathyayini is the sixth form of Navdurga.She embodies the values of sharing and caring. Young girls pray to Devi Kathyayini for a good husband.

Seventh Form:

Kaalaratri is the seventh form of Navdurga.Kaalaratri represents the deepest rest so that you can attain dynamism.

Eighth Form:

Maha Gauri is the eigth form of Navdurga.Gaura varna means white color. White represents purity. Purity comes out of innocence. Maha Gauri is the combination of brilliance and innocence

Ninth Form:

Siddhidhatri is the ninth form of Navdurga.When you want something, and if before the want arises it is available to you, that is called Siddhi (when you receive before you even feel the need, and when you receive more than what you need). A sadhak or seeker will get many siddhis on the path. However, if you misuse or run behind them, they will be lost.
